Mussoorie

Popularly known as the Queen of Hills, this charming hill station, 34 kms from Dehradun is situated at an altitude of 2003 mts in the Garhwal hills. above sea-level. Major Hindu pilgrimage sites like Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, Yamunotri, Haridwar, Rishikesh are not far from this place.

Mussoorie is a hill station which has pleasant climate around the year. Greenery is at its full bloom during September to November. The best seasons to visit Mussoorie is between April to June and again during September to November, when the plains of North India experience heat waves.

HOW TO REACH

 BY AIR
Jolly Grant is the nearest airport to Mussourie. It is located at a distance of 24 kilometers from Mussourie and is linked to all the major cities of India.

 BY RAIL
The nearest railway station is in Dehradun ,around 35 km from Mussourie. Many taxis’ and cabs are available outside the railway station.

 BY ROAD
The most convenient mode of transportation to visit Mussourie is by road. It has a good network of Highways and National Highways that connects Mussourie to all the major cities and towns of India.

 WHAT TO SEE

 Gun Hill
Enjoy a Ropeway ride to the second highest peak of Mussoorie. It can also be negotiated by bridles path which forks-off from Mall Road near Kutchery and to reach takes about twenty minutes. The Ropeway distance is only 400 mtrs.

 Camel's Back Road
It starts from Kulri Bazar near Rink Hall and ends at Library Bazar covering a total distance of about 3 kms. The main charm of this froad is horse riding and wlking. Sunset view of the Himalayas is superb.

 Kempty Fall
15 kms from Mussoorie on the Yamunotri Road having an altitude of 4500 ft. It has the distinction of being the biggest and prettiest waterfall located in a beautiful valley and is surrounded by high mountains.
